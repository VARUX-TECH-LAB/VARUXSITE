Bounded Autonomy for Maritime Operations

ABYSS is a concept architecture for unmanned maritime platforms where autonomous behavior is not trusted blindly. Autonomy is strictly allowed only inside validated mission contracts, risk thresholds and human authority boundaries.

Mission Contracts

Every mission is represented as an explicit contract defining objectives, allowed behaviors, restricted zones, abort conditions and authority requirements.

Operational Envelopes

Autonomous decisions are constrained by validated envelopes covering location, sensor confidence, environmental state, platform health and communication status.

Human-Governed Authority

ABYSS supports autonomy without removing human command authority from critical decisions, escalation paths or mission termination protocols.

ABYSS Reference Architecture

LAYERED BOUNDARY ENFORCEMENT

L1

Mission Contract Layer

Defines allowed mission objectives, zones, constraints, abort rules and escalation requirements.

L2

Sensor Fusion Layer

Combines sonar, radar, GPS, inertial state, environmental signals and platform telemetry into a unified operational picture.

L3

Risk Validation Layer

Evaluates whether current state remains inside the mission envelope using confidence thresholds, anomaly signals and environmental risk.

L4

Autonomy Boundary Layer

Restricts autonomous decision-making to approved behaviors and blocks actions outside validated mission boundaries.

L5

Human Authority Layer

Routes critical or uncertain decisions to human review, remote command or mission abort procedures.

L6

Evidence & Audit Layer

Records mission state, decision paths, risk transitions, overrides and abort conditions as durable evidence.

Safety Model

ABYSS is built around the assumption that autonomy will face uncertainty, degraded sensors, unstable communication and ambiguous environments. The architecture is designed to reduce authority under uncertainty, not expand it.

Sensor Confidence

If the platform cannot trust its sensor picture, available autonomy must narrow.

Communication State

Loss of communication changes the authority model and may trigger restricted behavior or mission abort logic.

Environmental Risk

Weather, current, obstacle density and navigation uncertainty affect mission envelope validity.

Abort Conditions

Predefined abort conditions prevent the system from improvising beyond approved operational boundaries.

Failure Modes Considered

DETERMINISTIC DEGRADATION

FAILURE MODE RISK ABYSS RESPONSE
Sensor Degradation Platform acts on unreliable environmental data. Narrow autonomy, request validation, restrict high-risk maneuvers.
GPS / Navigation Drift Platform location confidence becomes unstable. Reduce mission authority and trigger localization recovery or escalation.
Communication Loss Human authority cannot be reached. Enter restricted envelope or abort depending on mission contract.
Conflicting Mission Signals System receives inconsistent objectives or constraints. Freeze non-critical actions and escalate.
Unexpected Obstacle Density Operational envelope becomes invalid. Recalculate risk state and restrict autonomous movement.
Platform Health Degradation Actuator, power or propulsion state becomes unreliable. Abort, surface, return or hold according to contract.
Unclear Human Command State Authority chain becomes ambiguous. Reject critical transitions until authority is verified.
Evidence Logging Failure Mission decisions cannot be proven later. Restrict or halt critical operations when durable evidence cannot be maintained.

Design Principles

FOUNDATIONAL LOGIC

1. Control Before Autonomy

Autonomous behavior must be constrained by explicit rules, context and mission contracts.

2. Evidence Before Trust

Every critical decision should produce durable evidence for later verification.

3. Authority Before Action

Critical transitions require clear authority, not assumed intent.

4. Degrade Safely

When uncertainty rises, autonomy narrows.

5. Boundaries Over Intelligence

More intelligence is not the objective. Safer, bounded behavior is.

The Ocean Does Not Forgive Ambiguous Authority.

ABYSS explores the control architecture required when autonomous systems operate in hostile, uncertain and communication-limited environments. The goal is not to make unmanned maritime platforms more independent at any cost. The goal is to keep autonomy governed, bounded and accountable.
